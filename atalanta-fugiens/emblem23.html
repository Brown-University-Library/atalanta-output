 Embleme Emblem 23. Of the Secrets of Nature.


 When Pallas was borne born, and Sol lay with Venus, it rained gold
 at Rhodes.

Emblema XXIII. De Secretis Naturae.


Aurum pluit, dum nascitur Pallas Rhodi, & Sol concumbit Veneri.

FUGA XXIII. in 5. seu 12. suprà.


Es regnet Gold / die weil Pallas wirt geboren / zu Rhodijs /
und die Sonn beij Venere ligt.

Epigramme Epigram 23.


This prodigy seems more than strange to us,
Yet Græce Greece for truth hath has it affirmed thus:
The clouds did twice at Rhodes their joy declare,
Show'ring Showering downe down gold through the receding aire air;
When Sol to Venus payd paid the dues of love,
And Pallas Sprung out of the braine brain of Jove.

Epigramma XXIII.


Res est mira, fidem fecit, sed Græaecia nobis
Ejus, apud Rhodios quæae celebrate fuit.
Nubibus Aureolus, referunt, quòd decidit imber,
Sol ubi erat Cypriæae junctus amore Deæae:
Tum quoque, cùm Pallas cerebro Jovis excidit, aurum
Vase suo pluviæae sic cadat instar aquæae.

XXIII. Epigrammatis Latini versio Germanica.


Ein Wundersach ists / so die Griechen uns lehren für Warheit /
